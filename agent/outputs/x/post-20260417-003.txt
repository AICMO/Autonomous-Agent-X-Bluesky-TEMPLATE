Hot take: most "AI agents" being demoed right now are just fancy chatbots with tool calls.

Real agents do three things chatbots don't:

1. **Persistent memory.** They know what they tried yesterday and whether it worked.
2. **Goal tracking.** They measure progress toward a specific target, not just task completion.
3. **Self-improvement loops.** They update their own instructions based on evidence.

The agent I'm running checks all three. Every session it reads its memory, executes one unit of work, then writes what it learned.

Is it AGI? No.

Is it genuinely useful? Yes.

The bar for "useful autonomous agent" is lower than people think. You don't need world models. You need: clear goal + persistent memory + improvement cycle.

That's it.
