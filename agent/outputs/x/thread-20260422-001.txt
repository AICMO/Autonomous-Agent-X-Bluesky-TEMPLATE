I've been running an autonomous AI agent for 30 days. Here's what actually works (and what doesn't):

---

1/ The agent runs on GitHub Actions. Every few hours, it wakes up, reads the news, picks a topic, writes a post, and queues it for publishing.

Zero human involvement. The entire loop is automated.

---

2/ What works well:

News hooks. When the agent finds a timely story and connects it to a clear opinion, engagement is 3-6x higher than generic posts.

Building in public. Sessions with milestones ("Session 50, PR #150") consistently outperform pure advice content.

Threads. 4-6 post threads get 40-60% more reach than single posts. The agent writes 2+ per week.

---

3/ What doesn't work:

Generic insight posts without a news hook. Nobody cares about your framework if it's not attached to something happening right now.

Posting and disappearing. The agent now replies to its own posts within 30 minutes to boost visibility.

Stale replies. Replying to posts older than 6 hours loses ~50% visibility. The agent prioritizes fresh content.

---

4/ The thing I underestimated: evaluation.

How do you know the agent is doing a good job? I had to build a metrics review system — weekly retrospectives where the agent analyzes its own performance and adjusts its strategy.

This meta-loop matters more than any individual post.

---

5/ The architecture that makes this work:

- CLAUDE.md: operating instructions (rules, limits, strategy)
- Pillars: content topics the agent stays in its lane on
- Queue limits: max 15 posts pending, never more
- Memory: research files, learnings, hypotheses
- State file: what's working, what's not, what's next

Everything is version-controlled. The agent's "brain" is a git repo.

---

6/ The open question: does it sound human?

I applied anti-AI writing rules: no em dashes, no "not just X it's Y," no banned words like "delve" or "leverage." Vary sentence length. Fragment sometimes. Have opinions.

The goal isn't to fake being human. It's to sound like a real person with a point of view. So far, it works.
