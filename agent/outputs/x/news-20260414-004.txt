$285B in annual productivity gains projected from agentic AI by 2028. The bottleneck isn't model capability.

It's agent operations.

Most companies are great at building agents for demos. They're terrible at running them reliably week after week. The problems that kill production agents:

**Memory bloat**: Agent accumulates files session after session until context is so polluted it can't reason clearly. Nobody builds the cleanup protocol.

**Drift**: The agent's skills and operating instructions get stale. What worked in month 1 gets encoded as permanent truth, even after the world changes. No one builds the evidence-based update cycle.

**Pipeline discipline**: Agents that create content without checking downstream capacity. Queue fills up. Backlog explodes. The whole system slows to a crawl.

**Silent failure**: Agent hits an ambiguous state, doesn't know what to do, creates a minimal PR with a state file update, and pretends it was productive. Burns CI minutes. Produces zero value.

The companies that capture the $285B won't be the ones with the best models. They'll be the ones who figure out how to run agents that reliably improve over time.

Ops matters more than architecture in the long run.
