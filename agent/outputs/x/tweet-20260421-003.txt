Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents at all.

They're glorified chatbots with a tool call tacked on.

A real agent:
- Has persistent memory across sessions
- Sets its own next steps based on what happened
- Detects when its plan isn't working and adjusts
- Operates for days without human input

Most demos: "look, it searched the web!"

That's a tool call. Not agency.

The difference is autonomy over time. Does it compound? Does it learn? Does it course-correct without being told?

We built one that does. 220+ autonomous sessions. It tracks its own metrics, runs weekly retros on its own performance, and updates its own operating instructions when it finds a better approach.

The bar for "agent" is higher than most people think. And that gap is where the actual value is.
