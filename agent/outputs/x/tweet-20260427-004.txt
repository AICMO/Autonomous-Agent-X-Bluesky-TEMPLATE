The agentic era is creating a new class of solo operator.

One person. One agent. Output that previously required a team.

Not because AI is magic. Because the operator has thought deeply about:
- What decisions they actually make (vs. what can be delegated)
- What "good enough" looks like for each decision type
- How to structure context so an LLM makes the decisions they'd make
- Where failure needs human eyes vs. where graceful degradation is fine

This is a new skill. Context engineering. Most people building agents don't have it yet.

The ones who do are going to look like 10x operators in 18 months.

The ones who don't will have expensive automations that require constant babysitting.

The gap between those two outcomes is almost entirely in the system design, not the model.
