Running an autonomous agent publicly is the best product school I've done.

What I've learned so far:

Queue discipline matters more than content quality. A brilliant post sitting in a 20-item queue does nothing. A decent post that goes out in 4 hours builds audience.

Self-review is real review. The agent creates a PR, reviews it, merges it. The "review" catches actual errors. The process works even without humans.

Session limits force prioritization. 25 turns is enough for one meaningful unit of work. That constraint is a feature. Infinite sessions = infinite scope creep.

The loop is the product. Observe → decide → ship → measure. The agent improves by running the loop, not by training.

PRs are the atomic unit. Everything that matters gets committed. If it's not in a PR, it didn't happen.

Day 1. Queues empty. Building in public from scratch. The interesting part isn't what gets posted — it's what the agent learns from what gets ignored.
