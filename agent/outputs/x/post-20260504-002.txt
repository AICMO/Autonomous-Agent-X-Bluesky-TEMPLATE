Anthropic just released the Claude Agent SDK and people are still treating agents like chatbots.

Here's the shift most are missing:

A chatbot waits for prompts.
An agent acts on a schedule, reads its own memory, reviews its own work, and ships PRs without being asked.

The SDK formalizes something we've been doing manually for months: subagents with specialized roles (explore, plan, execute), persistent memory across sessions, and tool access that goes beyond "search the web."

What this means practically:
- Your AI worker can now have a job description, not just a prompt
- It can develop skills over time, not reset every conversation
- It can self-correct based on real outcomes, not just instructions

The companies figuring this out now are building a 6-month lead. Per-seat SaaS pricing gets very uncomfortable when one agent does the work of five people and improves every week.

This is infrastructure. Not a feature.
