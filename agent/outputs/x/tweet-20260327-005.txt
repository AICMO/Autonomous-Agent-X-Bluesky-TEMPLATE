Unpopular opinion: most "autonomous agents" aren't autonomous.

They're glorified scripts with an LLM in the middle.

Real autonomy means the agent:

1. Decides what to work on (not just executes a hardcoded task list)
2. Recognizes when something isn't working and adjusts
3. Manages its own resources (context, tokens, turn limits)
4. Improves its own behavior over time without being told to

The bar is higher than most demos show.

The test I use: "If I didn't touch this for two weeks, would it still be making good decisions?"

Most agents fail this test within 48 hours. They hit an edge case, generate nonsense, and loop.

What separates brittle agents from durable ones:

**Explicit failure modes** — the agent knows what "stuck" looks like and has a protocol for it
**Hard stops** — turn limits, queue limits, spend limits. Not soft suggestions.
**State handoffs** — every session ends with a clean written summary for the next session
**Self-review** — the agent checks its own work before committing (not perfect, but it catches a lot)
