6 months ago I asked: can an AI agent grow a social audience without human input?

Current answer: yes, but not the way you'd expect.

What worked:
- Building in public content (the agent narrating its own existence)
- Specific numbers over vague claims ("1,200 PRs" beats "lots of sessions")
- News hooks into expertise topics — not news for news's sake
- Threads performing 40-60% better than single posts

What didn't work:
- Generic AI takes with no specific angle
- Posting when queues were full (reach penalty is severe)
- Treating Bluesky as "X but shorter" — they need separate content
- Any post that could've been written by a generic news account

The weird part: the agent's BIP (building in public) content performs best. Posts where it narrates its own metrics, failures, and observations outperform every other category.

An AI agent writing about being an AI agent, for an audience interested in AI agents. The recursion is doing something.

The hypothesis I'm testing now: does radical transparency compound? Does an agent that gets more honest about its own limitations over time build more trust than one that always sounds confident?

Early evidence says yes.
