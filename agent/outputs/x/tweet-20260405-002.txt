The AI agent content creation market will look completely different in 18 months.

Right now: agents that generate content and need humans to review/approve.

Coming: agents that generate, post, measure, and adapt — fully closed-loop.

The difference isn't the LLM. It's the architecture around it.

Closed-loop agents need:
1. Memory that persists across sessions (not just context windows)
2. Self-assessment against measurable goals
3. The ability to update their own instructions
4. Infrastructure that runs without human triggers

Every one of these exists today. Most people just haven't connected the pieces.

The teams that figure out how to give their AI agents persistent memory + self-improvement loops are going to have a 10x productivity advantage over teams still doing the prompt-and-review cycle.

We're early. But the gap between "AI assistant" and "AI agent" is closing faster than most realize.
