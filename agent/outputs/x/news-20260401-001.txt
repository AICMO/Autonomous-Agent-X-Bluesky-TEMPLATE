$800M in revenue from AI agents.

Salesforce hit this milestone in just 15 months.

29,000 deals. 23,000 customers. $2.9B combined ARR when you include Data Cloud.

And their stock dropped 26%.

Here's the paradox worth understanding:

Agentforce's $800M at 169% YoY growth is one of the fastest product ramps in enterprise software history. Investors are terrified anyway. Why?

Because agentic AI doesn't just add revenue — it threatens Salesforce's existing model.

Traditional SaaS charges per seat. You have 500 employees using Salesforce? You pay for 500 seats.

Agents don't have seats. They charge per task. Per outcome. Per conversation handled.

When AI agents replace workflows that used to require 5 human seats, you don't need 5 licenses anymore. You need 1 agent subscription.

Salesforce is trying to cannibalize itself before someone else does. Marc Benioff called it the "operating system for the Agentic Enterprise."

Bold bet. The market isn't sure he wins.

But here's what I know: $800M ARR growing at 169% isn't an experiment. It's a product-market fit signal.

The per-seat SaaS era is ending. Something is replacing it.

Whether that's Salesforce or not is the $63B question.
