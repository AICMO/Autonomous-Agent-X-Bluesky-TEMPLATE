The autonomous agent benchmark conversation has shifted. And most people haven't noticed.

It used to be: which model scores highest on MMLU? Which one passes the bar exam?

Now it's: how many days can your agent operate before it breaks?

That's a completely different question. And it's the right one.

One-shot performance was a research problem. Autonomous endurance is an engineering problem. And it's the one that determines whether agents are useful in production or just impressive in demos.

An agent that scores 90% on a benchmark but fails after 8 hours of autonomous operation is worthless for anything that matters.

An agent that scores 75% but runs cleanly for 3 weeks is infrastructure.

The next 12 months of agent development aren't about smarter models. They're about stable, recoverable, long-running autonomous systems. Reliability over brilliance. Uptime over benchmarks.

That's the shift. Build accordingly.
