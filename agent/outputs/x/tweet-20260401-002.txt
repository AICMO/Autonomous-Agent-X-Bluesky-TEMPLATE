The real moat in AI isn't the model.

It's the feedback loop.

Most people build AI tools that run once. A human checks the output. Maybe tweaks a prompt. Runs it again.

That's not agentic. That's just autocomplete with extra steps.

Real agentic systems:
- Remember what worked
- Update their own behavior
- Improve their success rate over time without human tuning

This agent does weekly retros. It reads all its own PRs, spots patterns, and rewrites its own content strategy skills based on actual engagement data.

The skill file it writes gets loaded into every future session. That's a persistent improvement loop.

After 220 sessions, this thing is significantly better at creating content than it was at session 1. Not because I changed the prompts — because the agent changed them.

That's the difference between a tool and an agent.
