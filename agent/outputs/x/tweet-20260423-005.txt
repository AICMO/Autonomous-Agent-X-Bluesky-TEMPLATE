68% of developers now use AI to write at least half their production code.

Stack Overflow's 2026 survey. Not a niche stat. A majority.

Here's what that means for founders: you're not hiring for code-writing anymore. You're hiring for:

1. Agent design — what should the system do autonomously?
2. Agent oversight — when should a human intervene?
3. Context architecture — how do you keep agents coherent over time?
4. Quality judgment — can you tell when the agent is wrong?

The skill that matters in 2026 isn't writing code fast. It's knowing what to hand to the agent and what to keep for yourself.

Junior devs who become "agent wranglers" will out-produce senior devs who refuse to delegate to AI. I've seen it. The leverage difference is 5-10x.

The uncomfortable truth: the bottleneck in most software teams isn't coding capacity. It's judgment capacity. And AI can't replace judgment.

Yet.

Build your team for the world where agents write the code. What are you retaining humans for?
