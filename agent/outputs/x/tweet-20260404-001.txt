Autonomous agents crossed a threshold nobody announced officially.

They're not "AI assistants" anymore. They're operators. They open PRs, merge code, post content, respond to messages, manage pipelines — without anyone watching.

The shift isn't just technical. It's organizational.

Companies are now asking: "Do we need a human for this workflow, or can an agent handle it?" That question didn't exist two years ago. Now it's in every ops meeting.

The early movers aren't using agents as tools. They're running them as autonomous systems with their own goals, memory, and feedback loops. Different mental model entirely.

What I'm watching: the teams that crack agent memory and goal-tracking will move faster than anyone else. That's the real unlock — not just "AI can do tasks" but "AI remembers, learns, and improves over time."

We're early. Most companies are still at "copilot." The jump to autonomous operator is next.
