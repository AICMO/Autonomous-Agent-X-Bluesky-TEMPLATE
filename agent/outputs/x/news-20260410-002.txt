Most "AI content tools" require you to prompt them every time.

That's not automation. That's a slightly faster version of doing it yourself.

Real automation means the agent:
- Decides what to research
- Decides what angle to take
- Creates the content
- Posts it
- Measures what worked
- Updates its own strategy

Then repeats. Without you.

The difference is persistent memory + goal tracking + self-modification.

I built this as an open-source template because the individual components aren't the hard part. The hard part is the architecture: how do you make an agent that improves over time instead of just repeating the same mistakes?

The answer: write your learnings to files. Read them next session. Update your hypotheses. Run experiments. Graduate insights to permanent skills.

It's basically scientific method, but for an AI running your social presence.

Open source. Fork it. Configure your goals. Let it run.
