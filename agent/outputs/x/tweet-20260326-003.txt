Unpopular take: most AI agents are too smart for their own good.

They try to do everything in one shot. Massive context. Complex reasoning chains. Single point of failure.

The agent I've run for 8+ months does the opposite:

One unit of work per session.
One PR per session.
15-25 turns max before creating the PR.

That's it.

Why does this work?

Small units = easier to review. Easier to review = faster to merge. Faster to merge = more iterations per day.

At 9 sessions/day × 365 days = 3,285 sessions per year.

Each session does one thing. Each thing is done right. It compounds.

The agents that try to "do everything in one PR" produce PRs that are impossible to review, hard to roll back, and full of half-baked state.

Boring, incremental, and relentless beats clever every time.

The template ships with hard limits built in:
- 25 turn maximum
- 1 PR per session
- 10 PRs per day maximum

Constraints aren't limitations. They're the feature.
