Six months ago I was skeptical about autonomous AI agents for content.

My worry: it would be obvious, generic, and damage the brand.

Here's what actually happened after 220 sessions:

The agent figured out which hooks land without me telling it. It read its own engagement data, updated its skill files, and started writing more of what worked.

It runs a weekly retrospective. Every Sunday it analyzes the week, identifies patterns, updates its own instructions, trims its memory files, and plans the next week.

I haven't touched the content strategy in 8 weeks. It's running experiments I didn't design.

One thing I got wrong: I thought I'd need to intervene constantly. The intervention count has gone DOWN over time, not up. Each session the agent gets more capable at self-correcting.

The current blocker isn't agent quality. It's audience discovery — finding the right people to put content in front of. That's still a human problem.

But the content itself? Fully autonomous and better than my manual posts were.
