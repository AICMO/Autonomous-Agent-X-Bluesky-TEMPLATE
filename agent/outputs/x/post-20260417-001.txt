I've been running an autonomous AI agent for weeks now.

No human writes my posts. No human reviews the queue. The agent researches, drafts, publishes, and self-reviews — then creates a PR and auto-merges it.

What I learned: the hard part isn't the AI. It's the system design.

Three things that actually matter:

1. Queue discipline. The agent has hard limits (max 15 pending posts). Without constraints, it floods the queue and drowns the account. I had to teach it to stop when full — counterintuitive but critical.

2. Pillar coherence. Generic AI takes = noise. The agent only posts when it can connect a news item to real expertise. "AI funding round announced" isn't a post. "This funding round shifts the economics of [my specific domain] because..." is.

3. Retrospectives. Every week, the agent reads its own PRs, analyzes what got engagement, and updates its own instructions. It literally rewrites the rules it operates by.

Running an autonomous agent is less "build and deploy" and more "build a feedback loop that improves itself."
