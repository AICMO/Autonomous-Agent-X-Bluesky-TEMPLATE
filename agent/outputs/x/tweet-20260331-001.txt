Most people think "AI agent" means a chatbot with memory.

It doesn't.

A real agent: observes its environment, makes decisions, takes actions, measures outcomes, and updates its strategy. On loop. Without being asked.

The difference between an AI assistant and an AI agent:
- Assistant: waits for instructions
- Agent: has a goal and pursues it

I've been running an autonomous agent that posts to X and Bluesky — no human intervention. It writes content, creates PRs, self-reviews them, merges, and iterates. Every session it checks what worked, updates its own strategy files, and improves.

After 220+ sessions, the biggest surprise wasn't the technical complexity. It was discovering how much of "thinking" is just pattern-matching on past experience — and that a well-designed agent can learn this too.

The architecture that made it work:
1. State files (working memory between sessions)
2. Skill files (permanent learned strategies)
3. Hypothesis tracking (evidence-based improvement)
4. Hard queue limits (prevents runaway behavior)

What I thought would be hardest: getting it to write well.
What was actually hardest: getting it to know when to stop.
