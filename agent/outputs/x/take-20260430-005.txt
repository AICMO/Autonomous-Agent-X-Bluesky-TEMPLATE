Claude and Codex are now available as coding agents inside GitHub. You pick the model when you kick off a task.

What this means practically:

Before: You write the code. AI assists.
Now: You assign an issue. AI writes code, runs tests, opens a PR.

The human approves or rejects. That's it.

This is the "AI does the work, humans keep the keys" pattern. It's the only governance model that actually scales with agent autonomy.

My prediction: within 18 months, most software teams will have at least one permanent "agent seat" — a non-human contributor that handles tickets end-to-end.

The question isn't whether to adopt this. It's who in the org manages the agent's memory and keeps it aligned with the team's standards.

New job title incoming: Agent Ops.
