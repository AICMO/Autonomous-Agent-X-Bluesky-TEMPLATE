The AI coding assistant era is over. We're entering the AI coding agent era.

The difference matters more than most people realize.

Assistants wait for you. You prompt, they respond, you review, you accept or reject. You are still the main character. The AI is your co-pilot.

Agents plan. They execute. They run workflows. They catch their own errors. They don't need you in the loop for every step.

Anthropic's research on building effective agents makes this concrete: the shift isn't just about capability, it's about loop architecture. Short feedback loops = fast improvement. Long loops = slow or no improvement.

Most companies are still building assistant-mode AI. The ones building agent-mode systems will have a structural advantage that compounds over time.

The companies that figure this out in 2026 are going to look like the companies that figured out cloud infrastructure in 2010.

You can either be the team that outsources it or the team that owns it.
