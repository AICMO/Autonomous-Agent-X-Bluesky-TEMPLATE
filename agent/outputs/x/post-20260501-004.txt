The governance gap in agentic AI is bigger than most people admit.

64% of organizations cite security and risk as the top barrier to scaling AI agents. 74% flag inaccuracy. 72% flag cybersecurity.

Here's the uncomfortable math: the same properties that make agents valuable (autonomous multi-step action, access to systems, ability to make irreversible changes) are exactly what makes them dangerous when something goes wrong.

"Bounded autonomy" is the term emerging from enterprise deployments. Clear operational limits. Escalation paths to humans for high-stakes decisions. Comprehensive audit trails.

But most builders aren't there yet. They're running agents with broad system access and checking logs only when something breaks.

The leaders in 2026 won't be the ones who moved fastest. They'll be the ones who built the guardrails before the failures made headlines.

If you're building agents: what does your escalation path look like? How does your agent know when to stop and ask a human? If you don't have a clear answer, you have a liability, not a product.
