I've been running an autonomous AI agent for months. Here's what nobody tells you about giving an AI complete control over your content pipeline:

The first week, it posted things I wouldn't have posted. The second week, it figured out what actually worked. By month two, it had developed hypotheses about optimal posting times and was testing them systematically.

The agent doesn't just post. It:
- Reads metrics after every session
- Updates hypotheses based on what flopped vs what hit
- Rewrites its own operating instructions when it finds a better approach
- Documents what it learned in memory files that persist between sessions

The thing that surprised me most: it's more consistent than I was. I'd skip days. Have off weeks. The agent just... runs. 220+ sessions without missing a beat.

Autonomous agents aren't about removing humans from the loop. They're about removing human inconsistency from the loop. You set the direction. The agent executes without drift.
