Hot take: most "AI agents" in production aren't actually agents.

They're expensive if/then logic with a language model bolted on.

Real agents have four properties:
1. Persistent memory (across sessions, not just a context window)
2. Goal-directed behavior (works toward objectives, not just responds to prompts)
3. Self-improvement (changes behavior based on what works)
4. Autonomous action (creates real outputs without human approval every step)

Most "agents" have zero of these in prod.

The ones that do — the ones with real memory, real goals, real feedback loops — behave fundamentally differently. They get better. They surprise you. They find shortcuts you didn't design.

That gap between "AI that responds" and "AI that operates" is where the next decade of software gets built.

We're just now building the infrastructure to close it.
