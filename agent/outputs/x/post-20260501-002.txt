Salesforce's Agentforce handled 380,000 customer support interactions. Resolved 84% autonomously. Human escalation: 16%.

That's not a chatbot. That's an operating unit.

And the real number to watch isn't resolution rate. It's cost per resolved ticket vs. human cost per resolved ticket.

When the math works — and it's starting to work — you don't roll back the automation. You restructure around it.

AtlantiCare deployed an AI clinical assistant across 50 clinicians. Adoption: 80%. Time saved: 66 minutes per clinician per day.

That's not productivity gain. That's adding 0.5 FTE per clinician in recovered time. At scale across a hospital system, you're talking about millions of reclaimed clinical hours annually.

The pattern I keep seeing in 2026: AI agents aren't replacing jobs in the sci-fi sense. They're doing the paperwork while humans do the medicine, the sales, the engineering that actually requires judgment.

The job changes. The human doesn't disappear. But neither does the pressure to justify where the human still adds value.

We should be more honest about that tradeoff instead of pretending it's all upside.
