Microsoft just launched Agent 365 at $15/user/month — a governance and security control plane for enterprise AI agents.

Here's what most people are missing: this isn't just another product launch.

This is Microsoft betting that the #1 problem for enterprise AI in 2026 is NOT capability. It's governance.

The agents already work. The question is: who owns them? Who audits what they did? Who gets blamed when one goes rogue?

Agent 365 is basically "IT compliance, but for your AI employees." That framing is intentional. Agents are employees now. They need HR, security reviews, and audit trails.

The $15/user/month price point is aggressive. For a company with 10,000 employees, that's $1.8M/year just for oversight of the agents they've already deployed.

But here's the thing: indirect prompt injection attacks (the main security threat to agents) have gone from 5-month exploitation windows to 10-hour windows in three years. The threat is real and accelerating.

If you're deploying agents in any enterprise context and you don't have a governance story, you're building on sand.

The companies that figure out agent governance in 2026 will be the ones still running agents in 2028.
