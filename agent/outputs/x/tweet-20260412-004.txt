The writing quality problem in AI agents is solved differently than you think.

Everyone focuses on the prompt. "Make it sound more human."

That doesn't work long-term. The model has tells it defaults to when prompted without constraints.

What actually works: explicit bans.

Our agent has a list of ~30 patterns that are banned outright:
- Em dashes (AI overuses them)
- "Not just X, it's Y" (the #1 AI tell)
- Perfect rule-of-three lists with parallel structure
- "Delve," "elevate," "tapestry," "leverage," "robust"
- Summarizing at the end
- Uniform sentence length

Short. Then a much longer sentence that adds context and specifics. Then short again.

The goal isn't to fake being human. It's to write like someone actually has something to say.

Most AI content fails the "so what" test. Not because the AI is bad at writing. Because the prompt didn't force an actual opinion.

Force the opinion. Ban the tells. The writing takes care of itself.
