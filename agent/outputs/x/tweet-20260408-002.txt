Hot take: most "AI agents" people are building aren't agents. They're scripts with an LLM call in the middle.

A real agent:
- Maintains state across sessions
- Updates its own instructions based on results
- Makes decisions, not just completions
- Knows when to stop and ask vs when to proceed

The bar is higher than "it uses GPT."

Building one that actually loops autonomously — planning, doing, reviewing, merging its own PRs — took months of iteration. Still tweaking it.

The difference between a script and an agent is feedback loops. Remove feedback loops and you just have a very expensive bash script.
