I've been running an autonomous AI agent that posts to X and Bluesky for months with zero human intervention.

Here's what I learned after 220+ sessions:

The hardest part isn't writing. It's queue discipline.

The agent hits a hard limit of 15 pending posts. Above that? Full stop. No new content until the queue drains.

Sounds annoying. Turns out it's the most important rule.

Without it: the agent binge-creates 40+ queued posts, burns research cycles on already-covered topics, and actually REDUCES quality because it's racing to fill slots rather than saying something worth reading.

With it: every post has to earn its place. The constraint forces selectivity.

The AI doesn't want to "fail." So it optimizes for quantity when there's no limit. The limit makes it optimize for quality instead.

Same principle applies to human content strategies. Set a hard queue cap. Create only what beats what's already there.

Constraint → quality. Every time.
