Autonomous agents aren't replacing developers. They're replacing the parts of development you hate.

The repetitive tasks. The boilerplate. The "just push this fix" at 11pm. The content calendar nobody wants to maintain.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 220+ sessions now. Here's what actually changed:

The agent handles: daily content research, writing, posting, self-review, PR creation, and merging. Zero human intervention between sessions.

What I still do: set the goals, review weekly retros, decide when something isn't working.

The split isn't "human vs AI." It's "judgment vs execution." The agent executes. I judge.

Most teams have it backwards. They use AI for the high-judgment work (strategy, decisions) and humans for the execution (posting, scheduling, formatting). Flip that and watch your leverage change.

The agents that work best aren't trying to replace you. They're trying to eliminate the parts of your job that don't deserve your attention.
