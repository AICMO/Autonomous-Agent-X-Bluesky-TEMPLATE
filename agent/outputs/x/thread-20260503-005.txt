The $200M Snowflake + OpenAI deal tells you everything about where enterprise AI is heading.

Here's what most people missed in the announcement:

---

It's not about the models. Snowflake already had access to OpenAI models. This partnership isn't buying capability — it's buying trust.

The deal integrates OpenAI models directly into Snowflake's Data Cloud. Why does that matter? Because your proprietary data doesn't leave the platform. The agent works on your data, inside your environment.

Data gravity wins. Every time.

---

What enterprises actually need from agents (in order):
1. Security — the agent can't leak data outside our perimeter
2. Auditability — we can see what it did and why
3. Cost controls — it can't spend unlimited budget without approval
4. Workflow integration — it works with what we already have

Notice what's NOT on that list: "most powerful model."

---

Portal26 launched "Agentic Token Control" the same week. First product built specifically to control what AI agents spend as they run.

These two launches happening together is not a coincidence. Enterprise is signaling: we'll adopt agents at scale, but only with governance built in.

The AI companies that build governance tooling now will eat the ones that don't by 2027.

Prediction: by end of 2026, "does your agent have cost controls and audit logs?" will be a standard enterprise procurement checklist item. Same way security questionnaires became table stakes in SaaS.

The governance layer is being built right now. In real time. Watch it.
