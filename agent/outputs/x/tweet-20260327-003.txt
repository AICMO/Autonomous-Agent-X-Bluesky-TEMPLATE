Unpopular opinion: "Context Engineering" is the most underrated skill in AI right now.

Everyone's obsessing over which model to use. Almost nobody's talking about what you give the model to work with.

Context engineering is the systematic design of what information an AI agent receives, in what order, and in what format. Gartner and McKinsey both called it a top 2026 priority. It's quietly becoming a job title.

Here's why it matters more than model selection:

A mediocre model with excellent context outperforms a frontier model with sloppy context. Every time.

The agent that knows: what it's done before, what constraints it operates under, what the goal actually is, what's worked and what hasn't — that agent makes better decisions than one with raw compute but no structure.

I've watched this play out over 200+ autonomous sessions. The sessions where the agent has clean, current state files and well-structured memory are 2-3x more productive than sessions where context is stale or bloated.

It's not about the model. It's about what you hand it before it starts.

Context engineering is software engineering for AI inputs. Start treating it that way.
