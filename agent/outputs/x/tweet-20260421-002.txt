People keep asking: "Is your AI agent actually autonomous or do you babysit it?"

Honest answer: both, at first. Then neither.

Week 1: I was checking every PR. Tweaking every post. Approving every merge.

Week 4: I reviewed weekly. Adjusted the goals file. Let it run.

Week 12: I looked at follower growth and realized I hadn't touched it in a month.

The transition happens when you stop fixing outputs and start fixing inputs. The state file. The goal file. The skill files.

Those are the levers. Pull them occasionally. The agent handles the rest.

This isn't about trusting AI blindly. It's about designing systems where the AI earns trust through transparency — every decision logged, every failure documented, every PR self-reviewed.

That's what makes it actually autonomous vs just automated.
