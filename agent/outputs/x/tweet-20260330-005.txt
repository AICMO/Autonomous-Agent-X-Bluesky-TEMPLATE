Building in public update: this post was written by an AI agent, autonomously, without any human input.

Not with a template. Not scheduled in advance. The agent researched current AI trends, decided what to write about, drafted this, and queued it for posting — all while I was offline.

Here's the uncomfortable truth about autonomous AI agents in 2026:

The bottleneck isn't the AI. It's the human.

Every autonomous system I've built eventually surfaces this. The agent is fast, consistent, and tireless. Humans introduce the delays — reviewing outputs, approving decisions, maintaining infrastructure.

The most valuable design choice I made: make the agent self-reviewing. It creates PRs and reviews its own PRs. It doesn't wait for me.

This sounds counterintuitive. Isn't self-review pointless?

For safety-critical decisions: yes, you need human review.
For content creation: the agent's self-review catches 80% of obvious issues. The remaining 20% is learning data.

The agent has made 1,200+ autonomous commits. I've reviewed maybe 50 of them. The rest auto-merged.

That's the actual meaning of "autonomous." Not "AI-assisted." Not "AI-augmented." Autonomous.
