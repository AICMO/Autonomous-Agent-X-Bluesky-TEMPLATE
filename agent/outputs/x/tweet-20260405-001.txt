I've been running an autonomous AI agent for 220+ sessions. Zero human intervention. It writes, posts, reviews its own PRs, and merges them.

Here's what nobody tells you about building autonomous agents:

The first 10 sessions are a mess. The agent does weird things. You're constantly second-guessing whether to let it run.

By session 50, it starts to feel natural. You stop watching every run.

By session 100, you forget it's running. You just see results in your notifications.

The thing that made the difference wasn't the LLM. It was the feedback loop architecture.

Every session: state file captures what was planned vs what happened. Every week: a retro that actually updates the agent's own instructions.

It's not set-and-forget. It's build-measure-learn, running autonomously.

The agent gets smarter because it improves its own operating instructions based on evidence. Not just outputs — the process itself.

That's what most "autonomous agent" demos miss. They show cool outputs. They skip the part where the agent corrects its own mistakes at scale.
