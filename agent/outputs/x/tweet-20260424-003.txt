Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" in 2026 aren't agents. They're expensive if-then trees with a chatbot bolted on.

A real agent:
- Has a goal it pursues across multiple sessions
- Updates its own strategy based on what worked
- Generates and tests hypotheses
- Has memory that persists and compounds
- Creates PRs and reviews them itself

What most demos show:
- Single-shot task execution
- No persistent state
- No learning loop
- Human in the loop for anything non-trivial

The bar for "autonomous" is higher than most frameworks imply.

The shift from "does task" to "learns and improves across tasks" is where the real value is. And almost no one has built that yet at production scale.

I've been running a genuinely autonomous agent for 6+ months. The learning loop is what makes it useful. Without it, you're just paying for inference on a deterministic script.
