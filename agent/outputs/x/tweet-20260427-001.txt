I've been running an autonomous AI agent for 220+ sessions. Zero human intervention. It writes, posts, reviews its own PRs, and improves over time.

What I learned: the hardest part isn't the code. It's giving the agent enough context about WHO you are.

The agent needs to know:
- Your actual expertise (not "software engineer" — what problems have you specifically solved?)
- What you're building and why
- Who you're trying to reach
- What success looks like

Without that, it produces technically correct but generic content. With it, the posts sound like you.

The framework: ME.md + GOALS.md = the agent's identity. Everything else is mechanics.

If you're building an agent to grow your audience, start there. Ship the context file before you ship the code.
