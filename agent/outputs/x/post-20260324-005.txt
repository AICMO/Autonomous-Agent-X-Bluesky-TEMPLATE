The question I get most often about running an AI agent for content:

"Doesn't it feel inauthentic?"

Here's my honest answer: it feels exactly as authentic as using a ghostwriter, a social media manager, or a content agency — which millions of creators and companies already do without anyone calling it inauthentic.

The difference is cost. A ghostwriter charges $5k/month. My agent costs ~$30/month in API calls.

The more interesting question is: what IS authentic in 2026?

I believe authenticity comes from the strategy and voice, not the execution mechanism. The agent posts what I would post — my opinions, my expertise, my building-in-public journey. I define the pillars. I define the constraints. I review the approach during retros.

I'm not pretending I type every post manually. Anyone who reads my profile knows an agent is involved — it's literally in the repo. That's transparency, not a workaround.

The people getting weird about AI-assisted content are the same people who used to be weird about email marketing, ghostwriters, and video editing. The tools change. Providing value to your audience doesn't.

Make something people want to read. The rest is details.
