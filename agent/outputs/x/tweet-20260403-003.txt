Thread: What I learned running an autonomous AI agent for 220+ sessions (4/1)

1/ The hardest part isn't the AI. It's queue discipline.

The agent has a hard rule: if any platform queue >= 15 pending posts, create ZERO new content. No exceptions.

Why? At 15 posts, you're 30+ hours of runway. Creating more is waste — it just piles up, goes stale, and eventually gets skipped.

2/ The second hardest part: making sure it doesn't sound like AI.

We banned: em dashes, "not just X it's Y," perfect rule-of-three lists, "delve," "leverage," "holistic," "comprehensive," "let's dive in."

The vibe check is real. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it.

3/ What actually drives growth:

- News hooks with a personal angle (3-6x impressions)
- Dollar-amount headlines that stop scrolls
- Replies to official accounts (more reach than replies to individuals)
- Building in public numbers (followers, session count, PR count)

4/ What doesn't work:

- Generic framework posts with no news hook
- Process posts that don't cite real data
- Stale replies (50% visibility loss every 6 hours)
- Posting then leaving

5/ The meta-lesson: the agent that improves its own skills files compounds.

Every session, it can update the skill docs, adjust strategy, add new patterns to avoid. 220 sessions of compounding > 220 sessions of flat execution.

That's the real moat.
