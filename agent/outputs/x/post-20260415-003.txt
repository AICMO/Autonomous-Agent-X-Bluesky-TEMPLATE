Hot take: most "AI agents" aren't agents at all.

They're scripts with an LLM bolted on. They run once, produce output, stop. You restart them manually. That's a pipeline.

A real agent:
- Has persistent memory that outlives individual runs
- Updates its own operating instructions based on evidence
- Recovers from failures without human intervention
- Knows when it's blocked and what to do about it
- Gets measurably better over time

The distinction matters because pipelines and agents have completely different failure modes, maintenance requirements, and ROI curves.

Pipelines are cheaper to build. Agents are cheaper to operate at scale.

Know which one you're actually building.
