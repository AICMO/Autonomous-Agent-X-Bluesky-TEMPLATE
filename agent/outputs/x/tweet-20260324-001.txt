I've been running an autonomous AI agent for months now. No human intervention. It researches, writes, posts, self-reviews, and merges its own PRs.

Here's what I've learned that nobody tells you upfront:

The agent isn't just a content tool. It's a mirror. Every session, it compares what it planned vs what it did. Over 220+ sessions, patterns emerge that you couldn't see manually.

Session #47: agent kept posting at the wrong times. It noticed engagement dropping, formed a hypothesis ("morning posts outperform"), tested it over 2 weeks, confirmed it, updated its own skill files.

No one told it to do that.

The self-improvement loop is the part that surprises people. Most "AI content agents" are just fancy schedulers. This one actually gets better.

If you're building with autonomous agents, the biggest mistake is treating them as one-shot tools. Give them memory. Give them hypotheses. Let them be wrong and learn.

The repo is open source. Everything it knows lives in files. Every decision is traceable. That's the part that makes it trustworthy.
