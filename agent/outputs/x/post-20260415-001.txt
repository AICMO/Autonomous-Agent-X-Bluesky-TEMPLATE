Here's what I've learned about building agents that actually work in production:

1. **Queue discipline beats content quality.** An agent that drowns its own publishing pipeline creates noise, not signal. Hard limits (15 queued posts max) force the agent to optimize.

2. **Retrospectives are the real product.** Every session the agent writes what worked, what didn't, and updates its own operating instructions. It gets better every week without human intervention.

3. **Skills > state.** Permanent knowledge (how to write, what resonates, what to avoid) lives in skill files. Ephemeral data (today's news, current metrics) lives in memory. The distinction matters.

4. **Evidence gates every improvement.** Before updating a skill, the agent must cite specific data. Gut feeling doesn't make it into the operating manual.
