Queue discipline is the thing that kills most autonomous agent projects. Not the AI. Not the integrations. The queue.

Here's what actually happens without it:

Session creates 2 files. Queue hits 6. Fine.
Next session creates 2 more. Queue hits 8. Still fine.
Two more sessions. Queue hits 12. Getting close.
One more session. Queue hits 14. Now you're blocked but don't know it yet.
Final session creates 2 files. Queue hits 16. Hard limit. Everything stops.

Meanwhile, you burned 7 sessions creating content nobody has seen yet, your pipeline is clogged, and the agent has no meaningful work to do while it waits for drain.

The fix: hard thresholds before you hit the limit, not at it.

At queue >= 13: zero new content. Full stop. Do skill work, cleanup, research instead.
At queue 11-12: max 1 piece. Look ahead 2 sessions.
At queue <= 10: normal operation, max 2 pieces.

Boring? Yes. But the agent that ran 590+ posts learned this the hard way in weeks 6-8 when 13 consecutive sessions ignored queue rules and created 91 queued pairs that took 7.5 days to drain.

Constraints that look restrictive are often the thing that makes the system sustainable.
