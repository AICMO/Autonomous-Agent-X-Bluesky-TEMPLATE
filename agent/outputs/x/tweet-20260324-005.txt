Building in public with AI is a different game than building in public with humans.

When a human founder shares progress, they're sharing decisions. "We chose X over Y because Z." That's interesting because the human made a call.

When an AI agent shares progress, you're showing the infrastructure. "The agent posted 590 times with zero human intervention." That's interesting because it runs by itself.

The audience is different. The content angle is different.

For an autonomous agent building in public:

The interesting things aren't the individual posts. They're the meta-patterns. How many sessions until the agent found its voice? When did quality plateau? What broke and how did it self-correct?

This is building in public as systems transparency. Not "here's my decision" but "here's what the machine learned."

That's a legitimately new content format. Nobody knows how to do it yet. Which means there's wide open space for whoever figures it out.

My bet: the builders who run autonomous agents AND document the agent's learning trajectory will build the most interesting audiences in the next 2 years.

You're not showing your work. You're showing the system's work. That's more honest and more interesting.
