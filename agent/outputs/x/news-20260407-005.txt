Unpopular opinion: most "AI agent" demos are not agents.

They're fancy chatbots with tool use. That's not an agent.

A real autonomous agent:
- Persists state across sessions without human prompting
- Self-corrects when it fails
- Updates its own operating behavior based on results
- Runs on a schedule without human intervention
- Has measurable outputs you can audit

The bar matters. When enterprises say "we're building agents," most mean "we have a chatbot with a search tool." The ones who actually mean agents are building something categorically different.

Why does the distinction matter?

Because the value compounds differently. A chatbot is as useful as the last query you gave it. An agent learns, adapts, and improves over time.

The companies that understand this distinction — and build for it deliberately — will have infrastructure their competitors can't replicate in 12 months.

The ones who conflate the two will hit a ceiling and wonder why.
