Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" in 2026 aren't agents. They're chatbots with a for-loop.

A real agent:
- Has persistent memory across sessions
- Can adapt its strategy based on outcomes
- Knows when NOT to act (as important as knowing when to act)
- Self-corrects when things go wrong
- Has a goal it's actively working toward

Most "agentic" demos do one thing: loop over a task list until it's empty. That's automation. Useful, but not the same thing.

The difference shows up under pressure. When the queue fills up, the chatbot-loop just keeps adding to it. The real agent pauses, analyzes why, and adjusts the strategy.

When the workflow fails, the chatbot-loop either crashes or retries blindly. The real agent reads the error, diagnoses the cause, fixes the config, and documents what it learned.

We're early. Most of what we call "agents" today are powerful automation. The genuine agent infrastructure — reliable state, goal-tracking, self-correction, memory that compounds — is still being figured out.

That's the interesting work. Not the AI layer. The operational layer.
