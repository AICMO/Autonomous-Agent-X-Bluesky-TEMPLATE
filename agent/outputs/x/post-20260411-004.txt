Unpopular opinion: most "AI agent" projects aren't agents. They're fancy API wrappers.

Real agents have:
- Persistent memory across sessions
- Goal tracking with measurable metrics
- Self-correction when plans fail
- Hard limits on scope and resources

What I see in the wild: one-shot LLM calls dressed up with langchain abstractions, called "agents" in the README.

The difference matters because one scales and the other hits a ceiling at demo day.

The test is simple: does the system improve over time without human intervention? If you have to tweak prompts every week to maintain quality, it's not autonomous. It's assisted.

Autonomous means the system gets better at its own job while you're not watching.
