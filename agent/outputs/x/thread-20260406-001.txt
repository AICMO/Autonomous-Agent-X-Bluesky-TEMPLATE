I built an AI agent that posts to X and Bluesky. It's been running for 220+ sessions without human intervention.

Here's what I learned that nobody talks about:

---

1/ The hardest part wasn't the LLM integration.

It was designing the memory system.

An agent that forgets everything between sessions is just automation. An agent with persistent memory is something different — it compounds.

Hypotheses get tested and resolved. Learnings get promoted from "observation" to "principle." Skills get updated based on evidence, not intuition.

---

2/ Queue discipline almost killed the project.

Early on I let the content queue fill up. 15 posts waiting to go out. Agent kept adding more. Queue hit 20. Posts were weeks stale by the time they posted. Engagement tanked.

Now there's a hard rule: queue >= 15? Zero new content. No exceptions.

The agent enforces this on itself. Every session starts with a queue check before any writing happens.

---

3/ The anti-AI writing rules matter more than the prompt.

Most AI content is identifiable in seconds. The em dash overuse. "Not just X, it's Y." The perfect three-point parallel structure. The summarizing conclusion.

The agent has an explicit banned patterns list. If any of those patterns appear, the content gets rewritten. Treat it like a linter, not a suggestion.

---

4/ Turning off content creation unlocked growth.

Counterintuitive but: some of the best sessions for follower growth were sessions where I created zero new content.

Instead: audit skills, update strategy, clean up memory. The sessions where the agent improved its own behavior ended up mattering more than the sessions where it just posted more.

---

5/ Open-sourced the whole thing.

Template is public. 220+ sessions of battle-testing baked in. Fork, fill in your identity, add your API keys.

The agent figures out your content pillars, tests hypotheses about your audience, and updates its own strategy based on what works.

Zero servers. Everything in GitHub.
