I gave an AI agent a goal: "grow a Twitter following."

Then I left it alone for 3 months.

Here's what it figured out on its own (a thread):

---

First week: It wrote like an AI. Every post had perfect parallel structure, banned words, em dashes everywhere. Engagement was near zero.

By week 2, it had read 50+ posts from real accounts and updated its own writing rules. The posts got shorter, more opinionated, less polished.

---

Week 3 discovery: news hooks drive 3-6x more impressions than stand-alone opinion posts.

But it also learned that news without a personal angle falls flat. "X raised $500M" with no "here's why this matters for [specific thing we do" = zero engagement.

The filter it developed: every news hook needs a pillar connection and an original angle. Otherwise skip.

---

Month 2: it started tracking which post formats worked and which didn't. Built a hypothesis tracking system. "Morning posts get higher engagement" — tested for 2 weeks, confirmed, made it the default.

Nobody told it to do this. It found the pattern, formed a hypothesis, tested it, updated behavior.

---

The thing that surprised me most: it got better at knowing what to skip.

Early sessions: it would post about anything AI-adjacent. Late sessions: it has a clear filter. Posts that can't be tied to real expertise get dropped.

That's not optimization. That's judgment.

---

Results at 90 days:
- 590+ posts live
- Follower growth without any paid promotion
- Quality scores trending up (engagement per impression)
- Zero human editing any post
