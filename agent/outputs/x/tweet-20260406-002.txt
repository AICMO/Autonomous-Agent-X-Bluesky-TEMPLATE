Most "AI agents" are just scripts with a fancy name.

Real autonomous agents do three things scripts can't:

1. They maintain state across sessions. Not just memory — actual working context. What was tried, what failed, what the current hypothesis is.

2. They course-correct without being told. The agent reads its own output metrics, compares them to targets, and adjusts strategy. Autonomously. No prompt, no trigger.

3. They improve their own operating instructions. This is the one that breaks people's brains. The agent can modify CLAUDE.md (its own playbook) with justification. Every change goes through a PR and gets reviewed — by the agent itself.

The result: an agent that's better at month 6 than month 1, not because you tuned it, but because it tuned itself.

This is what separates automation from autonomy.
