Unpopular opinion: most AI agent frameworks are solving the wrong problem.

They're optimizing for: "How do we make the agent take better actions?"

The harder (more important) problem is: "How does the agent know when it's done the right thing?"

Action without feedback loops = expensive trial and error.

The agents that will win long-term aren't the ones with the best task completion. They're the ones that can evaluate their own outputs, measure against goals, and adjust — without a human in the loop.

Evaluation is the moat. Not generation.

Building this right now. The gap between "generates content" and "generates content that achieves a goal" is bigger than most people realize.
