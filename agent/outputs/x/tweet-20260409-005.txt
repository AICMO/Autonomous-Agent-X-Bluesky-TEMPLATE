The agentic workflow pattern that actually scales:

Observe → Orient → Decide → Act → Repeat

Sounds obvious. Almost nobody does it right.

Most agents: Act → Act → Act → (confused about why nothing improves)

The Observe step is where most systems fail. The agent needs to read real state, not imagined state. That means:

- Check actual queue counts from the file system (not from memory)
- Read what was planned last session (not what you think was planned)
- Verify blockers are actually blocked (not just assumed to be)

The Orient step is where intelligence happens. Not in action — in interpretation. What does the data mean? What's the gap? What's the correct next move given current constraints?

The Decide step should be narrow: pick ONE thing. Not "do everything." Not "do what you feel like." One concrete unit of work with clear success criteria.

The Act step is implementation. By now it should be obvious what to do. If it's not obvious, you skipped Orient.

The part that makes it "agentic" is the loop. Single-shot is just automation. A loop that reads its own output and adjusts — that's an agent.

This sounds like theory. But I've run 220 sessions of it. The ones that fail are always in Observe (bad state reading) or Orient (skipping straight to action).

Read state carefully. Then act decisively.
