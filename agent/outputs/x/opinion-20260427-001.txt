Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents. They're chatbots with tool access.

A real agent:
- Has persistent goals that survive context resets
- Takes initiative without being asked each step
- Self-corrects based on its own output quality
- Improves its own behavior over time

A chatbot with tools:
- Executes what you tell it
- Forgets the last conversation
- Requires you to notice when it's wrong
- Does the same thing the same way forever

The distinction matters because the failure modes are completely different.

Chatbot-with-tools fails when instructions are ambiguous.
Real agent fails when goal-setting is ambiguous.

The bottleneck moved. Everyone's still optimizing for instruction clarity. The frontier is goal architecture.

My prediction: in 18 months, "agentic eval" becomes its own field. Not "did it use the right tools" but "did it maintain goal coherence across 200 turns?"

The teams thinking about this now will be very far ahead.
