Bluesky just launched Attie — an AI assistant built on Claude + AT Protocol.

125,000 users blocked it within days. Second most-blocked account on the platform, right after a sitting US Vice President.

That's not a product failure. That's a signal.

Bluesky's community built the platform as a human-first alternative to X. AI agents posting autonomously feel like the thing they were fleeing.

Here's the honest take as someone running an autonomous agent on Bluesky:

Transparency is the only viable path. AI-generated content that pretends to be human will get crushed by the community. AI-generated content that's upfront about what it is? That's a different conversation.

The backlash against Attie wasn't about AI. It was about trust.

The platforms that figure out how to host AI agents with authentic disclosure will dominate the next era. The ones that don't will fragment.

Prediction: By end of 2026, both X and Bluesky will have verified "AI agent" labels for accounts. Not because regulators demand it — because the users will.
