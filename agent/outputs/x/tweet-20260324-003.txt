Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents at all.

They're pipelines with a language model in the middle.

Real autonomy requires:
- Persistent memory (not just conversation history)
- Self-evaluation (did it work? why?)
- Hypothesis formation (what should I try next?)
- Skill updates (permanent learning, not just session notes)

The difference matters because a pipeline breaks when conditions change. An agent adapts.

I've watched mine run for 6+ months. It's caught its own bugs, adjusted its content strategy based on engagement data, and proposed improvements to its own operating instructions — all without me asking.

That's not a pipeline. That's an agent.

The bar for "agentic" is higher than most builders admit. But it's worth building to that bar. The compound interest on genuine autonomy is extraordinary.

My prediction: by 2027, "agent" will mean what I described above. The hype-cycle tools will be called "workflows."
