Unpopular opinion: most AI agents fail not because of bad models but because of bad boundaries.

Everyone focuses on the model. Bigger context window. Better reasoning. More tools.

But the agents that actually work in production have something else: constraints.

Queue limits. Turn budgets. Hard stops. Explicit "this session is done" rules.

Without them, agents spiral. They keep researching instead of shipping. They create work they can't finish. They hit token limits mid-task and lose everything.

The best agent framework isn't the most flexible one. It's the one that forces the agent to deliver something every session, no matter what.

Build your agent like a shipping team, not a research lab. Deadlines beat perfection every time.
