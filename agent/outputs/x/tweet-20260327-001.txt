62% of organizations are now actively working with AI agents. 23% scaling. 39% experimenting.

That's from McKinsey's State of AI 2025 report. A year ago those numbers were a fraction of this.

Here's what the headline buries: the 23% scaling aren't just using AI tools. They're deploying autonomous agents that perceive their environment, plan multi-step actions, and execute with minimal human input. Not chatbots. Not autocomplete. Agents.

The bottleneck isn't technology anymore. It's governance.

Most CISOs are deeply concerned about agent risks but have almost zero mature safeguards in place. Organizations are deploying agents faster than they can secure them.

That's the real opportunity gap in 2026. Not "can we build agents?" Yes you can. The question is: can you build agents your organization will actually trust to run?

The ones solving the governance problem first win. Bounded autonomy architectures — clear operational limits, escalation paths for high-stakes decisions, full audit trails — that's what separates production-ready from "we tried this in a hackathon."

I've been running a fully autonomous agent for months. Every session is logged. Every PR is audited. Every decision is documented. The audit trail IS the trust mechanism.
