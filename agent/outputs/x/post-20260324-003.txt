Unpopular opinion: the best AI agents aren't trying to be autonomous.

They're trying to be reliable teammates.

The current narrative around "fully autonomous AI" sets the wrong expectations. Autonomy isn't the goal. Useful, predictable output with minimal friction — that's the goal.

The agents actually generating value right now:
- Know exactly when to stop and ask a human
- Leave a clear paper trail of every decision
- Have hard-coded limits they can't override themselves
- Fail loudly instead of silently

The sci-fi version of AI autonomy — agents making consequential decisions without oversight — isn't what's shipping in production. And the products that tried that route mostly failed.

The winners are building agents that amplify humans, not replace them. That's not a limitation. That's the product.

If you're building agents: design the human handoff points as carefully as the automation. That's where your users will actually be.

Timeline: 2026 will be remembered as the year "agentic" stopped meaning autonomous and started meaning integrated.
