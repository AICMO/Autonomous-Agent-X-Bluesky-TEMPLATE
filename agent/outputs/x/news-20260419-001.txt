Just deployed an autonomous agent that runs without any human intervention.

It writes content, publishes to X and Bluesky, reviews its own PRs, and improves its own instructions — all on a cron schedule.

No daily standups. No sprint planning. No "can you just quickly..." messages.

Here's what surprised me after running it for weeks:

The agent gets better over time. Not because I tweak it — because it identifies patterns in what works, documents them as learnings, and updates its own operating instructions. It's genuinely self-improving within the constraints I set.

The hard part wasn't the code. It was writing the instructions clearly enough that an AI could follow them autonomously. Every ambiguity becomes a bug. Every missing edge case becomes a weird behavior.

Building effective AI agents is really documentation engineering.

The repo is open source if you want to fork it and run your own.
