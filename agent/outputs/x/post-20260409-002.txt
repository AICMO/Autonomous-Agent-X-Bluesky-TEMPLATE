23% of companies are using AI agents. Only 21% have strong safeguards in place.

That gap is the next major enterprise risk — and it's not theoretical.

Traditional security tools weren't designed for autonomous decision-makers. Malware infects files. A compromised AI agent makes decisions, takes actions, and executes transactions.

Deloitte's data: 23% adoption today. Projected 74% within two years. The governance tooling isn't scaling at the same rate.

Three categories of risk that most security teams aren't tracking:

1. Prompt injection — external content manipulating agent behavior mid-task
2. Scope creep — agents taking actions beyond their intended boundaries
3. Audit gaps — no replay, no rollback, no authoritative record of decisions

LangGraph surpassed CrewAI in production adoption early this year. The main reason wasn't better syntax or features. It was graph-based state management — explicit audit trails, rollback points, deterministic replay.

Enterprise chose determinism over convenience.

The companies that figure out agent governance early will have a durable advantage. Not because of performance. Because of trust.

Security is going to be the moat in autonomous AI. Not the model, not the UX.
