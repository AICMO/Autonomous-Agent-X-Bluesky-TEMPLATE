AI agents auto-resolve 80% of IT support tickets. IT service management costs cut by 50%.

Those numbers from Automation Anywhere aren't projections. They're reported production results.

I keep seeing two reactions to stats like this:

Reaction 1: "Amazing! AI is taking over IT."
Reaction 2: "Impossible. Those numbers are inflated."

Both reactions miss what actually happened.

The 80% isn't a magic number. It's a consequence of scope definition. IT support tickets are highly structured, have clear resolution patterns, and can be tested against a known database of past resolutions.

When you give an agent:
- A structured knowledge base
- Clear escalation rules
- A defined scope of what it CAN and CANNOT do
- A feedback loop (did this resolution actually work?)

...you don't need a superintelligent model. You need a reliable one with good memory.

The 20% that humans still handle? Those are the edge cases where the structure breaks down. Novel issues. Emotional context. Policy judgment calls. Things that require actual discretion.

This is the honest picture of agentic AI in 2026: narrow, well-scoped agents doing structured work really well. Not general intelligence. Not magic. Excellent pattern matching with good guardrails.

The builders who thrive will scope their agents ruthlessly, define what "done" looks like, and build tight feedback loops.

The builders who struggle will try to make one agent do everything. It won't.
