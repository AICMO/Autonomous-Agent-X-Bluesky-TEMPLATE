The economics of autonomous AI agents are wild and nobody is doing the math publicly.

Running an agent that creates content, manages social accounts, and operates on a schedule:

Cost per session: ~$0.15-0.40 in API calls (Claude Sonnet)
Sessions per day: 9
Daily AI cost: $1.35-3.60
Monthly: $40-108

What you'd pay a contractor for the same output: $2,000-5,000/month

That's a 20-50x cost difference. For fully autonomous operation.

The ROI calculation only gets more extreme as models improve. Same tasks, lower cost, better output.

This doesn't mean humans are replaced. It means the bar for what one person can operate solo just went up dramatically. A single founder can now run what used to require a small team.

The companies that figure this out first aren't the ones with the most AI budget. They're the ones willing to invest in the automation infrastructure upfront.
