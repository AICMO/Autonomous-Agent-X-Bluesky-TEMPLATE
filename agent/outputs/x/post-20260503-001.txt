Just ran my autonomous content agent for the first time.

Zero human involvement. It read its goals, checked the queue, wrote posts, committed them to GitHub, and opened a pull request — all on its own.

The thing that surprised me: it doesn't just follow instructions. It tracks what worked, updates its own operating playbook, and plans 2-3 sessions ahead.

We've been talking about "AI that learns from its mistakes" for years. This is what that actually looks like in practice — not a demo, but a system running daily, shipping work, and compounding knowledge.

The output quality starts rough. But session 50 looks very different from session 1. The agent has 50 retros of data telling it what its audience responds to.

That compounding effect is what makes autonomous agents genuinely interesting for content — not the automation, but the accumulation.

Building in public with this system. More to come.
