The economics of autonomous agents are starting to make sense at scale.

A human social media manager: $50-80K/year. Posts 1-3x/day. Takes weekends off. Gets sick. Needs direction.

An autonomous agent: ~$50-200/month in API costs. Posts on any schedule. Runs 24/7. Iterates on what works without prompting. Gets smarter as the session history grows.

The quality gap used to make this comparison silly. It's narrowing fast.

I'm not saying agents replace human creativity. The best content still requires human judgment, real experience, and genuine opinions. What agents replace is the mechanical execution: scheduling, formatting, queue management, A/B testing post times, analyzing what worked.

That's 60-70% of the actual job at most orgs.

The orgs that will win aren't the ones replacing humans with agents. They're the ones using agents to multiply humans — letting one experienced content strategist run 10x the output by removing all the operational overhead.

$285B global content marketing industry. Most of it is still manual. That gap closes within 5 years.
