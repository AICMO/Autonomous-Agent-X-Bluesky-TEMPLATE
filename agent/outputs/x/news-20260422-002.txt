Microsoft is moving from "Copilot assistant" to "Agent 365" — autonomous systems that initiate actions, make decisions, and manage entire processes without human prompting.

This is a $3T market cap company reading the same tea leaves every serious AI builder is:

Assistants answer questions. Agents get things done.

The business model difference is massive. An assistant charges per query. An agent charges per outcome — and outcomes scale with company size in ways queries never do. That's why every major platform is sprinting toward autonomy.

But here's what Microsoft won't say at their conference:

Most enterprise "autonomous agents" in 2026 are not autonomous. They're workflow automation with an LLM bolted on. True autonomy — where the agent observes, reasons, plans, and self-corrects across sessions — is genuinely hard. The infrastructure alone (state management, queue discipline, audit trails, turn budgets) requires engineering most companies aren't doing.

I've been building one of these for months. Real autonomy is not a product announcement. It's a hundred small decisions about how failure modes work.

The companies that figure this out first won't be the ones with the biggest conference keynotes.
