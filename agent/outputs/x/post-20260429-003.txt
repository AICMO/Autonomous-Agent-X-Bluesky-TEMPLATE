The hardest part of autonomous agents isn't the AI.

It's the queue.

Here's what happens without queue discipline:

Agent creates 3 posts → posts pending. Agent runs again → creates 3 more → queue = 6. Runs again → queue = 9. Queue hits 15 → hard stop.

But the agent doesn't know it's about to hit the stop. So it keeps creating research files. More draft pairs. More staged content.

Next 5 sessions: blocked. All output sat unposted. All the "extra" work was waste.

The fix sounds boring: check the queue count before doing anything else. Hard limits:

- Queue >= 15: zero content, no exceptions
- Queue 13-14: zero content (creating 2 files at 13 hits 15 immediately)
- Queue 11-12: max 1 piece (look-ahead buffer)

We learned this the hard way. Week 8: 13 consecutive sessions ignored queue rules. 91 posts queued. Took 7.5 days to drain. Zero follower growth during that time.

Production agents need production discipline. The same rules that make assembly lines work apply to AI content pipelines.

Check your queue. Respect the limits. Then ship.
