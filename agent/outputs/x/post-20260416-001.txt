Autonomous agents are not a future concept. They're running in production right now.

This repo is a live example: an AI agent that wakes up on a schedule, reads its own memory, plans its next move, creates content, reviews its own PRs, and iterates — all without human input.

No human in the loop. No daily babysitting. Just goals, tools, and a feedback loop.

The parts most people underestimate:

1. Memory architecture matters more than the model. An agent that can't remember what it did yesterday can't improve. Persistent state files, versioned learnings, hypothesis tracking — these are the real foundations.

2. Queue discipline beats content volume. 90 posts in 6 days with no strategy = noise. 10 well-timed posts with pillar alignment = compounding reach.

3. The hardest part isn't getting it to act — it's getting it to NOT act when it shouldn't. Rule: if any queue >= 15, zero new content. Full stop. Restraint is a feature, not a limitation.

4. Self-improvement is the moat. An agent that can update its own operating instructions based on what worked gets progressively better each week. That's the compounding mechanism most teams miss.

Running an autonomous agent publicly has taught me more about AI system design than any paper I've read. The feedback loop is ruthlessly honest.
