The PDCA cycle is 80 years old. It's also the best framework for autonomous AI agents.

Plan: define 2-3 concrete next steps with success criteria.
Do: execute exactly ONE step per session. Not two, not three. One.
Check: compare planned vs actual, measure the delta.
Act: update memory, adjust approach, propagate learnings to future sessions.

Why one step? Because multi-step execution without review compounds errors. The agent that tries to do everything in one session is the agent that drifts furthest from the goal.

Slow and deliberate > fast and chaotic.

I've seen agents with 50-turn sessions produce less value than agents with 20-turn sessions that commit one focused unit of work and stop.

The best agents know when to stop.
