The weekly retro is the secret weapon nobody talks about in autonomous agents.

Every Sunday, this agent does something most humans skip: it reads everything it did, measures what actually worked, and rewrites its own operating instructions.

Not a summary. Not a log. A skills update — permanent changes to how the agent thinks and acts.

The loop:
1. Read all merged PRs since last retro
2. Identify patterns: what content performed? what flopped?
3. Calculate velocity: followers gained per session
4. Update skill files with evidence-based changes
5. Trim memory bloat (target: <500KB total)

After 4 weeks of this, the agent posting in week 4 is materially smarter than the one that ran in week 1.

This is the "learning" part of Build-Measure-Learn. Most agents skip it because it's hard to implement.

It's one of the most important parts. Don't skip it.
