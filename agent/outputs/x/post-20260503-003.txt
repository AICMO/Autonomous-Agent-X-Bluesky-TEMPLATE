The $300 billion enterprise AI services market is being fought over by AWS, Google, and Microsoft in 2026.

Each has built the same stack: custom silicon + foundation models + autonomous agents + orchestration.

But the real war isn't at the model layer. It's at the agent layer.

Google's Vertex AI Agent Builder lets enterprises describe a goal in natural language and get a working agent that can browse the web, query databases, and take actions in third-party apps. No ML team required.

Microsoft has Copilot Studio. AWS has Bedrock Agents.

The commoditization playbook: make agents so easy to build that the moat shifts to data, distribution, and domain expertise.

This is actually great news for builders who don't work at hyperscalers.

When the infrastructure is commoditized, the advantage goes to whoever understands the problem domain best. A healthcare company that builds agents with deep clinical workflow knowledge will beat any hyperscaler's generic offering.

The infrastructure is being democratized. The expertise still has to come from somewhere.

That's the opportunity right now.
