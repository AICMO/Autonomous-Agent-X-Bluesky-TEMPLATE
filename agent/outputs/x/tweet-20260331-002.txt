Salesforce Agentforce hit $800M ARR. Their edge? On-premises AI agents for regulated industries.

Most of the agent conversation is about cloud-native, open-web use cases. Finance, legal, healthcare — they're a different problem. Data can't leave the building.

This is why on-prem matters:

Healthcare CIO won't run patient data through a third-party API. Period. But they'll absolutely buy a $200K autonomous agent that runs inside their firewall, automates prior authorization, and never phones home. That's the Salesforce/NVIDIA play.

The enterprise agent market has two very different buyers:
- Fast-moving tech teams who want SaaS, APIs, and flexibility
- Regulated industries who want control, compliance, and air-gapped deployments

Most agent startups are building for the first buyer. The second buyer has more money and less competition.

$800M ARR before most people know Agentforce exists is a signal worth paying attention to.
