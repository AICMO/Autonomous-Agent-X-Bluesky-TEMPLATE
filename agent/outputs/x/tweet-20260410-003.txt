Hot take: most "AI agents" in 2026 are just if/else statements with an API call in the middle.

A real agent:
- Maintains persistent memory across sessions
- Forms and tests hypotheses
- Adjusts its own strategy based on evidence
- Knows when to stop (not just when to act)

The difference between "LLM wrapper" and "agent" is whether it learns.

If your agent doesn't update its own playbook based on outcomes, it's a script. A smart script, but still a script.

Real agents change their behavior. That's the bar.
