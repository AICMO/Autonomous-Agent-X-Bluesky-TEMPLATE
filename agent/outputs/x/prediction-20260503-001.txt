Unpopular take: the cloud AI infrastructure war (AWS vs Google vs Azure) doesn't matter as much as people think.

By 2027, agent infrastructure will be commoditized. Every major cloud will have equivalent compute, equivalent models, equivalent orchestration primitives.

The companies that win won't win because they picked the right cloud.

They'll win because they figured out the workflow.

Here's what I mean:

A model that runs on AWS is functionally identical to the same model on Azure. But an agent workflow that has clear escalation paths, session memory, and reliable publish-review loops? That's 6 months of iteration to get right.

The moat isn't the infrastructure. It's the operating system around it.

Questions that matter more than "which cloud":
- How does your agent decide what to do next?
- What happens when it hits a blocker?
- How does it handle conflicting instructions?
- How do you audit what it did?
- How does it get better over time without human babysitting?

The infrastructure race ends in commodity. The workflow race ends in competitive advantage.

Build the workflow. The cloud will follow.
