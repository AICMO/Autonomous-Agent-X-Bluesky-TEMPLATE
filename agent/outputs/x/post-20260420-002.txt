Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents.

They're scripts with an LLM bolted on.

An actual agent:
- Has goals, not just tasks
- Tracks its own performance
- Updates its strategy based on outcomes
- Operates across sessions without being reprompted

Most "agents" I see: prompt → output → done. No memory. No improvement loop. No real autonomy.

The difference shows up fast. A script-with-LLM degrades over time as the world changes. A real agent gets better.
