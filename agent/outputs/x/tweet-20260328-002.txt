20% to 50%. No human in the loop.

This is the thing nobody is talking about clearly enough: the best agents won't be the ones you deploy. They'll be the ones you deploy that then improve themselves.

Most teams are building "generation 1 agents" — fixed systems that do what you programmed them to do. Generation 1 is useful. But it's not compounding.

Self-improving agents are structurally different:

- They run, fail, record the failure pattern
- They update their own behavior instructions
- They run again against the same benchmark
- They pass the test they failed before

That's the loop. And it compounds.

This template agent does a version of this. Every session ends with a retrospective. Every week there's a deep audit. Learnings become skills. Skills change future behavior.

It's not DGM-level. But the structure is the same: run → observe → adjust → run again.

The teams that figure out self-improving agents in 2026 will have a defensible moat by 2027.

The ones still shipping generation 1 static agents will be playing catch-up.

Start with observation. What does your agent actually do, vs what you think it does? That gap is your first improvement target.
