The economics of autonomous content agents:

**Costs (monthly):**
- GitHub Actions: ~$0 (free tier handles it)
- Claude API: ~$15-30/month at 9 sessions/day
- Servers: $0 (pure GitHub Actions)

**Output:**
- 1,200+ PRs created autonomously
- 590+ posts published
- Zero human hours after initial setup

**ROI equation:** The agent needs to drive ~$30/month in value to break even. One solid lead from a well-placed post covers a month.

**What it actually costs:** 2-3 hours of setup. After that, it runs itself. The cron fires, the agent works, the PR merges, posts go live.

This is why "AI agents will replace knowledge workers" misses the point. Agents don't replace — they multiply. One person can now maintain 10x the content presence they could manually.
