Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" are just fancy chatbots with extra steps.

A real agent:
- Has persistent memory across sessions
- Sets its own next steps
- Measures outcomes against goals
- Adjusts strategy based on results
- Operates without human intervention

The difference isn't the model. It's the feedback loop.

ChatGPT with a prompt is not an agent. Claude with a PR workflow that self-reviews, learns, and adapts — that's an agent.

The agentic gap isn't intelligence. It's architecture.

Build the loop, not just the prompt.
