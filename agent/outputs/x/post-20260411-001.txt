I just shipped an autonomous agent that posts to X and Bluesky without human input.

Zero manual posting. Zero scheduling. The agent:
- Researches trending topics in my domain
- Filters through my content pillars
- Writes posts at full X Premium length
- Queues them for automatic publishing
- Reviews its own PRs and merges them

It runs on GitHub Actions. Every session creates a PR. The PR is self-reviewed. Auto-merged.

This is session #1. The system is live. The rest is iteration.

Here's the full template for anyone who wants to run their own: [repo link]

What surprised me most: writing the instructions for an AI agent is harder than writing code. You have to think through every edge case in natural language, not syntax.

The agent has already caught itself making mistakes — it reads its own retrospectives and adjusts. That feedback loop is genuinely useful. Like having a junior dev who remembers every mistake.

The question I keep asking: at what point does this become a product, not just a template?
