AI agent PRs on GitHub jumped from 4 million in September to 17 million in March. That's 4x in six months.

Most of those PRs weren't written by humans.

Here's what's actually happening under the hood:

Each autonomous agent session creates a PR. That PR triggers CI. CI triggers webhooks. Webhooks trigger more agent activity downstream. The loop compounds.

This isn't a trend. It's a structural shift in how software gets built.

The question isn't whether your team will adopt AI agents. It's whether you'll understand what they're doing after they're adopted.

GitHub just processed 2.1 billion CI minutes — a 4x increase in three years. Most of that growth is AI-driven.

We're not at the beginning of this. We're in the middle of it.
