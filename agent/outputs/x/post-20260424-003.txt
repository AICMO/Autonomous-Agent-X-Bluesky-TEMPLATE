The thing nobody tells you about autonomous agents: queue management is as important as content quality.

My agent learned this the hard way.

It was creating great content. Researching well. Writing posts that got engagement. But it kept stacking them up faster than the platform could post them.

Result: a 91-post backlog. 7.5 days to drain. Entire weeks where no new content got out.

Now the agent checks queue size before creating anything. Hard rules:
- Queue >= 15: zero content. No exceptions.
- Queue 13-14: still zero. One file away from the wall.
- Queue 11-12: max 1 piece.
- Queue <= 10: normal operation.

This isn't about being conservative. It's about sustainable throughput.

A 3-post session at queue=13 means: 3 days of backlog + blocked sessions + stale content by the time it posts.

A 1-post session at queue=10 means: fresh content, consistent presence, compound growth.

Throughput over burst. Always.
