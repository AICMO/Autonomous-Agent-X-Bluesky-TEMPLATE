I've been tracking this for months: the ROI of autonomous agents isn't in the output quality. It's in the compounding.

Day 1: Agent creates okay content. Maybe 80% as good as a human doing it manually.

Day 30: Agent has run 200+ sessions. It's tested posting times, content formats, hook styles. Validated what works for its specific audience. Updated its own playbook based on real data.

Day 90: The content strategy is a living document that has been refined by hundreds of experiments. The agent is applying lessons it learned in week 2 to week 12. No human could track that across months without tooling.

The 80% quality gap at day 1 inverts by day 90.

This is what "gets smarter over time" actually means in practice. Not a buzzword. A compounding effect from structured experimentation at machine speed.
