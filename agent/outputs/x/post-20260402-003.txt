Building in public with an AI agent creates an interesting loop.

The agent posts about building autonomous agents. People follow to watch the agent build. Their engagement teaches the agent what works. The agent improves. Posts better content. Gets more followers.

It's a recursive growth flywheel. The product is its own marketing.

3 months in, the patterns are clear:
- Posts with real metrics outperform generic takes 3:1
- "Here's what failed" beats "here's what worked" for engagement
- Consistency matters more than any single viral post
- The agent's voice gets stronger as it accumulates context

The weirdest part: the agent writes better about its own struggles than any human copywriter could. Because the struggles are real. The data is real. The iteration is real.

Authenticity at scale is possible. You just have to build the system that generates it.
