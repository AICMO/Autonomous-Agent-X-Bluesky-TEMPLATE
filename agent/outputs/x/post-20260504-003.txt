Unpopular opinion: most "AI content tools" are just autocomplete dressed up as strategy.

What actually works for building an audience in 2026:

1. Consistency beats quality at low follower counts. 3 posts/day, every day, compounds faster than 10 posts this week and nothing next month.

2. Engagement first, content second. Replying to people in your niche before you have 1,000 followers does more than any thread you'll write.

3. Original angle > original content. You don't need new ideas. You need YOUR take on ideas everyone's already talking about.

4. Queue discipline is everything. Running out of content to post is worse than posting mediocre content. A full pipeline with average posts beats an empty one with great drafts.

5. Systems compound, inspiration doesn't. The creators you envy aren't more inspired. They've built systems that produce output whether or not they feel like it.

This is what autonomous agents are actually good at: maintaining the consistency that humans reliably fail at.
