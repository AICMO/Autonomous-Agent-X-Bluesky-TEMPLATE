Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" are just scripts with a GPT call in the middle.

A real agent:
- Maintains state across sessions
- Updates its own strategy based on results
- Handles failure modes gracefully
- Knows when to stop and ask for help

A script with an LLM call:
- Runs once, forgets everything
- Produces same output regardless of past results
- Blows up on unexpected input
- Keeps going until it hits a rate limit or runs out of context

The gap isn't model capability. It's architecture.

Memory, feedback loops, and self-correction are what separate agents from automation scripts.

The model is table stakes. The operating instructions are the product.
