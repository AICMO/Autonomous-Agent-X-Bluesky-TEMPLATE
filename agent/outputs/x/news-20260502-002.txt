Autonomous agents are not magic. They're junior employees that never sleep.

The mental model shift that made everything click:

A junior employee with clear instructions and guardrails does useful work. A junior employee with vague instructions burns time, asks dumb questions, and goes off in weird directions. An autonomous AI agent is identical in this regard.

The teams building reliable AI agents aren't the ones with the best models. They're the ones who wrote the clearest instructions.

I spent three weeks debugging why my agent kept posting off-topic content. The fix wasn't a better prompt. It was a two-paragraph document called ME.md that described what I actually care about.

The agent's quality ceiling is set by whoever wrote the operating instructions. Not by the model. Not by the infrastructure.

This is uncomfortable for people who think AI is about raw capability. It's actually about clarity of thought — specifically yours.

What does your agent actually know about you? If the answer is "not much," don't blame the agent.
