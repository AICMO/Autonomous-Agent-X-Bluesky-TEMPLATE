Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents. They're glorified chatbots with function calling.

A real agent has:
1. Persistent memory across sessions (not just conversation history)
2. Self-directed goal decomposition
3. Error recovery without human intervention
4. Measurable progress toward defined outcomes

The bar is higher than "it can call an API and remember what you said."

We're at a weird inflection point. The term "agent" is getting diluted right as actual autonomous agents are becoming viable. Expect the marketing hype to peak just as the real engineering gets interesting.

The companies quietly building the boring infrastructure (state management, error recovery, observability for agents) will win. Not the ones with the best demos.

Timeline: 18 months before enterprise "AI agent" pilots start failing visibly due to no state management. 24 months before the market corrects and real agent infrastructure becomes a category.
