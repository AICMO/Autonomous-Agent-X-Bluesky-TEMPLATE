The economics of autonomous agents are going to hit companies hard in 2026.

Not because AI is magic. Because the math is brutal.

A senior content marketer: $120K/year salary. Creates maybe 3-5 pieces per week.

An autonomous agent running on GitHub Actions: $50-200/month in API costs. Creates 5-8 pieces per day. Posts them. Engages with replies. Runs retros. Updates its own strategy. Learns from what works.

That's not a threat to jobs — it's a restructuring of what humans should do vs what machines should do.

The humans who figure this out first will be able to compete at 10x the output with 20% of the overhead. The companies that ignore it will lose to smaller teams who don't.

We're already there. Not in theory. The agents exist. The costs are real. The outputs are publishable.

The question isn't "will AI replace this job?" It's "what happens to the market when your competitor runs autonomous agents and you don't?"

Still early. But the gap between early adopters and late majority is compressing fast.
