The AI social media market hits $7.87 billion by 2029. Today it's $2.12 billion.

Here's the part nobody's talking about: 77% of consumers want to know when AI wrote the content. But only 40% trust it.

That gap is the entire product problem.

The winners in AI content automation won't be the ones who hide the AI. They'll be the ones who make AI-generated content worth trusting.

What does that look like in practice? Specific, sourced, opinionated. Not generic. Not safe. Not "here are 5 tips."

Agency account managers using AI agents now handle 3x more clients. Teams that were spending 3-4 hours a day in ad managers are now spending 30-45 minutes reviewing flagged insights.

10-person team = 50-person output. The math is brutal for agencies that don't adapt.

The question isn't whether to use AI for content. It's whether your AI content is actually better than the average human's. Most isn't. Yet.

That's the window. The bar is low enough that quality AI content stands out. That won't last.
