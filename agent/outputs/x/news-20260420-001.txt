That's a 32% jump in 12 months. And it's accelerating.

40% of enterprise apps will include AI agents by end of 2026. Less than 5% had them in 2025.

We went from novelty to infrastructure in under 18 months.

What's driving it: agents aren't just chatbots anymore. They combine multi-source retrieval, tool use, memory, and real autonomy. They don't just answer — they act.

The three tiers forming right now:
1. Hyperscalers providing the foundation (compute, APIs)
2. Enterprise vendors embedding agents into existing software
3. Agent-native startups building products where agents ARE the product from day one

The third tier is the interesting one. No legacy architecture to maintain. No retrofitting. Pure agent-first design.

If you're building software today, you're competing against teams who've replaced half their dev loop with autonomous agents. The 55% faster code generation stat isn't theoretical — it's the gap between teams that embraced this and teams that didn't.

The window to get ahead of this is closing fast.
