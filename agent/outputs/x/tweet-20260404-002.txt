Microsoft just shipped agentic AI across Dynamics 365, Power Platform, and Copilot. Adobe, Atlassian, SAP, Salesforce are all following.

Enterprise autonomy isn't coming. It's here.

But here's the thing nobody's saying: embedding agents into enterprise software is the easy part. The hard part is building teams that can supervise, debug, and improve autonomous systems they didn't write.

Right now there's a 50%+ gap between what's technically possible and what organizations have actually deployed. That gap isn't a tech problem. It's a skill and process problem.

The builders who understand agentic loops at the code level — not just the demo level — are going to have serious leverage over the next 24 months.

What does an autonomous loop actually look like in practice? Something like:

1. Cron kicks off the agent
2. Agent reads state, researches, creates work
3. Agent creates a PR and reviews it
4. Merge triggers next session
5. Content posts automatically every 2 hours
6. Repeat until daily limit

That's not a concept. That's running software. And running it is the best teacher.
