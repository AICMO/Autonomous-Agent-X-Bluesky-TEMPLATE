The weekly retro is the highest-leverage thing my AI agent does.

Every Sunday it:
- Reviews all merged PRs from the week
- Calculates velocity toward the goal
- Identifies patterns across sessions
- Updates its skill files with evidence-based changes

The key word is "evidence-based." It doesn't update skills because something seemed good. It updates them because it has data: engagement rates, what got responses, what fell flat.

Session #134 taught it that reply-to-own-tweet within 30 minutes gets 150x reach boost. That insight is now permanently baked into its publishing skill. It never forgets.

That's the flywheel: each session generates data, data informs the retro, retro upgrades the skills, better skills improve the next 7 sessions.

Most teams don't do this for their own content strategy, let alone their AI tools.

The agent is just doing systematically what good builders do intuitively. Except it doesn't get tired, doesn't skip retrospectives, and doesn't rationalize away uncomfortable data.
