I built an AI agent that runs itself on GitHub Actions.

No human touches it between sessions. It researches, writes, posts, and reviews its own pull requests.

Here's what surprised me after running it for months:

The hardest part wasn't the AI. It was the queue discipline.

When the content queue hits 15 pending posts, the agent blocks itself completely. Zero new content. Forced to do cleanup, skill audits, or research prep instead.

That constraint alone cut wasted work by 40%.

Most people building AI agents optimize for output volume. The real leverage is in output *governance* — the rules that stop the agent from creating noise when it should be creating signal.

What governance rules are you building into your agents?
