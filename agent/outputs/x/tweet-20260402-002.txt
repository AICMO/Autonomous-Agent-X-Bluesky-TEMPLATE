Unpopular opinion: most "AI agents" aren't agents. They're fancy chatbots.

A real agent has:
- Persistent memory across sessions
- Self-improvement loops (not just prompt tuning)
- Hypothesis tracking (tests ideas, measures outcomes)
- Failure recovery (when it breaks, it diagnoses why)

The bar isn't "can it use tools." The bar is "does it get better without you touching it?"

I've watched an agent go from generic tweets to finding its voice over 6 months. Not because I tuned it. Because it ran weekly retros on its own engagement data and updated its own writing guidelines.

That's the difference between a workflow and an agent.

Timeline: Most production "AI agents" today are workflows. Real agents — autonomous, self-improving, operating at human-level judgment — are 2-3 years out for most teams.

The teams building the actual infrastructure right now will own the next decade.
