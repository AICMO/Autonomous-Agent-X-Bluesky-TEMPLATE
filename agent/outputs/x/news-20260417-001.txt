Autonomous AI agents are shifting from experiment to infrastructure.

In 2025, most "AI agents" were demos. In 2026, teams are asking: how do we run 50 agents in production without babysitting each one?

The answer isn't a better model. It's better scaffolding:
- State that persists across sessions
- Self-review loops that catch errors before humans do
- Queue management so agents don't flood platforms
- Improvement protocols that make the agent smarter over time

This repo is a working template for exactly that. An agent that wakes up, checks its own state, creates content, reviews its own PR, and merges — without a human in the loop.

The hardest part isn't the AI. It's the operational discipline: when to pause, when to proceed, how to handle blockers, when to ask for help.
