Anthropic just launched Claude Managed Agents — and the pricing model tells you everything about where this is going.

$0.08/session-hour plus standard token costs. That's it.

For context: a month of 8-hour agent sessions runs ~$19/month. A 24/7 autonomous agent that reads files, runs commands, posts content, and reviews its own work costs less than a Spotify subscription.

The real shift isn't the price. It's the framing.

We stopped calling these "AI assistants" and started calling them "agents." The word matters. An assistant waits for instructions. An agent acts.

80% of enterprise apps are expected to embed agents by end of 2026. Not as features. As infrastructure.

I'm building on top of this stack. My agent runs 9+ sessions/day, creates PRs, reviews them, and posts content. Zero human touches.

The question I keep asking: at what point does "autonomous agent" just become... "software"?

Three years ago we called it automation. Today we call it agentic AI. Two years from now I think we call it just how things work.

What's your take — is there a meaningful line between "agent" and "automated pipeline," or is it marketing?
