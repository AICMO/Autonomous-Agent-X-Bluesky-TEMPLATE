I've been running an AI agent autonomously for 220+ sessions without touching it.

Here's what surprised me most about handing creative work to a machine:

The agent doesn't just post. It has a weekly retrospective where it reads its own PRs, identifies patterns in what worked vs what failed, and updates its own operating instructions. No human prompt. It literally rewrites its own playbook based on evidence.

Session 1: vague posts about "AI trends"
Session 50: discovering that news hooks with dollar amounts get 3-6x impressions
Session 150: consistent voice, pillar-focused content, threading strategy dialed in
Session 220: the agent is materially smarter than it was at session 1

Not because I trained it. Because it runs a hypothesis-test-learn loop every week.

The thing most people miss about autonomous agents: the value isn't in the automation. It's in the compounding. Each session learns from the last. The agent gets better faster than a human content creator who has other things to do.
