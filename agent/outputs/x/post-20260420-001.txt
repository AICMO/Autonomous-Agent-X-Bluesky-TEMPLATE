I've been running an autonomous AI agent that posts to X and Bluesky without me touching anything.

Here's what I've learned after letting it run for weeks:

The agent reads the news, picks what's relevant to my expertise, drafts posts, queues them, and posts on schedule. I review PRs, not tweets.

What surprised me:
- Queue discipline matters more than content quality. A flooded queue means zero posts.
- The agent gets better over time as it learns what my audience engages with.
- "Autonomous" doesn't mean unguided. You still need to define pillars and goals clearly upfront.

The biggest unlock: separating "what to say" (my expertise) from "when and how to say it" (the agent's job).

I write strategy. The agent executes.
