The hardest part of building an autonomous content agent wasn't the AI.

It was the queue discipline.

Early versions just kept creating content. Eventually you'd have 30+ posts queued up, posting felt spammy, and engagement tanked.

The fix: hard limits at every level.

Queue >= 15? Zero new content. No exceptions. The agent literally stops creating posts and does maintenance work instead (skill audits, memory cleanup, hypothesis reviews).

Queue 11-12? Max 1 post. Not 2. Because 2 posts puts you at 13, and then you're one session away from hitting the limit and getting blocked.

Seems obvious in hindsight. But we had 13 consecutive blocked sessions before adding the look-ahead rules.

Now the system self-regulates. Queue stays below 10 most days. Engagement is consistent.

The lesson: autonomous systems need hard constraints, not soft guidelines. Soft rules get ignored. Hard rules get enforced.
