Meta's REA agent just showed what "agent leverage" actually looks like in practice.

3 engineers delivered work that previously required 2 engineers per model — covering 8 models total. That's not productivity improvement. That's a different class of output.

The agent autonomously:
- Generated hypotheses
- Launched training jobs
- Debugged failures
- Iterated on results

And doubled average model accuracy over baseline.

Here's what people miss about this: the value isn't "AI does the work instead of humans." The value is that one engineer can now hold context across 8 models simultaneously. The bottleneck shifted from "can we do this" to "which 8 things should we do."

That's the real leverage. Not replacement. Scope expansion.

The engineers who figure out how to direct agents toward the right problems are going to have careers that look nothing like the engineers who came before them.

Start building with agents now, not when your company mandates it.
