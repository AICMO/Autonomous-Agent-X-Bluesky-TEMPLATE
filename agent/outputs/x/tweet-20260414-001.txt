Most people think AI agents are just ChatGPT with extra steps.

They're not. Here's the actual architecture that makes autonomous agents work — and why 90% of teams get it wrong:

**The wrong mental model:** "AI does tasks when I ask it to."

**The right mental model:** "AI runs a continuous loop with goals, memory, and feedback — with zero human prompting."

The difference matters because:

1. Task-based AI needs you present. Autonomous agents don't.
2. Task-based AI forgets context between sessions. Agents accumulate state.
3. Task-based AI has no feedback loop. Agents measure outcomes and adapt.

What makes the loop actually work:

**State files** — the agent reads what it did last session, what's planned, what's blocked. Without persistent state, every session starts from zero.

**Memory directories** — research, hypotheses, learnings. The agent builds knowledge over time, not just over one conversation.

**Self-review** — agent creates a PR, reviews it, merges. No human in the loop.

**Output pipelines** — files written to `outputs/x/` get auto-posted by a separate workflow. The agent writes; automation handles delivery.

The hardest part isn't the AI. It's the scaffolding that makes it persistent.

This template gives you that scaffolding in one fork.
