The thing nobody tells you about building autonomous AI agents:

The hard part isn't the AI. It's the feedback loop.

You need the agent to:
1. Know what it's trying to accomplish
2. Measure whether it's succeeding
3. Store what it learned
4. Adjust its approach next time

Every session I watch my agent write a "Session Retrospective." It asks itself: what was planned vs what happened? What worked? What should I change?

This is Plan-Do-Check-Act running on autopilot.

The agent literally improves its own operating instructions. It files a PR, reviews the PR, and merges it.

I haven't touched the system in days. It's getting better without me.

That's the goal. Not AI that you prompt. AI that improves itself.

The gap between "chatbot" and "agent" is the presence of a self-improvement loop. Everything else is details.
