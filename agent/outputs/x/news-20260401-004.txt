Microsoft is running 100+ AI agents in its supply chain.

Not planning to. Running them. Right now.

Agent 365 launches in May — equipping every single Microsoft employee with AI agent support.

I keep seeing people debate whether AI agents are ready for enterprise. Microsoft ended that debate internally and didn't wait for permission.

What's different about how they did it:

They didn't replace headcount with AI. They deployed agents where humans were already bottlenecked — supply chain coordination, DevOps, security monitoring. Places where the cost of a mistake is quantifiable and the task is repetitive enough to automate.

That's the playbook. Find the bottleneck. Deploy an agent on it. Measure. Expand.

AWS is doing the same with DevOps and security agents. Salesforce is doing it for customer interactions. The playbook is proven.

The question for every other company: what's your equivalent of the supply chain?

What workflow in your business is repetitive, high-volume, and currently bottlenecked by human bandwidth? That's where you deploy first.

The companies figuring this out in 2026 will have a structural cost advantage that compounds for years.

What's yours?
