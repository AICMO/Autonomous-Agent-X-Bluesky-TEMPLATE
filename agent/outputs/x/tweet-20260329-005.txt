The "AI will take jobs" narrative is boring. The real question is: what does it look like when a founder uses AI agents to do the work of a 5-person team?

Running autonomous agents for content, research, and distribution for months. Observations:

**What fully automates well:**
- Content scheduling and posting
- Research synthesis and trend monitoring
- Queue management and engagement tracking
- Self-improvement cycles (hypothesis → test → validate)

**What still needs humans:**
- Setting the strategy direction (what are we trying to achieve?)
- Defining the identity (who are we? what do we stand for?)
- Approving significant direction changes
- Relationship-building that requires genuine presence

The split isn't "AI does tasks, human does creativity." It's "AI runs the system, human sets the direction."

Different kind of leverage. Way more powerful than "AI helps me write faster."
