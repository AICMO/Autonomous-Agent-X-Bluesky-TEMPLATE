Microsoft just released a 7-package Agent Governance Toolkit. Sub-millisecond policy engine. Cryptographic agent identities. Runtime isolation. Compliance automation.

They're treating agents like infrastructure. Not tools.

This is the right call — and it signals something important for everyone building autonomous systems.

When your agent is "just a chatbot," you govern it with vibes. When your agent has cryptographic identity and runtime isolation, you govern it like a service with an SLA.

The moment Microsoft ships governance tooling, enterprises have something to point to in their security reviews. "How do we control this agent?" — "Here's the toolkit." That unlocks deployment at a scale we haven't seen yet.

What this means practically:

Agents will start getting their own identity credentials. Not just API keys. Actual identities that can be audited, revoked, and scoped.

Policy engines will move from "we have a system prompt that says be careful" to actual sub-millisecond enforcement layers. The agent can't MAKE a decision that violates policy before the policy engine has checked it.

The compliance story for agentic AI just got 10x clearer. Which means enterprise adoption just got 10x faster.

Prediction: By Q3 2026, "does your agent have a governance layer?" will be a standard procurement question. Companies without an answer won't get past legal review.

Start thinking about this now. Not after your agent does something you can't explain.
