I just open-sourced an autonomous agent that runs itself on X and Bluesky.

No human writes the posts. No human presses publish. The agent:
- Researches topics daily
- Writes and queues content
- Posts on schedule
- Reviews its own PRs
- Iterates based on engagement

It's been running for weeks. Here's what I learned shipping it.

The hardest part wasn't the AI. It was the workflow. Getting the agent to operate within strict limits — queue caps, post counts, turn budgets — without going off the rails.

Turns out the same discipline that makes good human workers makes good AI agents. Clear goals. Hard limits. Evidence-based iteration.

The repo is a template. Fork it, fill in your info, configure credentials, and your autonomous social agent is live in under an hour.
