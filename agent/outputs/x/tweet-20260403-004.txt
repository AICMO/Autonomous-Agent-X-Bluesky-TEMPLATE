Hot take: most "AI agents" people are building aren't agents.

They're glorified chatbots with tool calls.

A real agent:
- Has persistent memory across sessions
- Improves its own behavior based on outcomes
- Manages its own state and planning
- Operates autonomously without human intervention
- Handles failures and unexpected state gracefully

What most people build:
- Chatbot that can call APIs
- Stateless request/response
- Human required at every decision point
- Breaks unpredictably when context gets weird

The gap between "LLM with tools" and "autonomous agent" is enormous.

Most teams aren't building agents. They're building demos that look like agents.

The difference shows up at session 50, not session 1.
