Unpopular take: most "AI content tools" are just expensive autocomplete.

Real autonomous content agents are different. They:

1. Track their own performance metrics session to session
2. Form hypotheses ("does X posting time correlate with engagement?")
3. Test the hypothesis over multiple weeks
4. Update strategy only when evidence confirms it
5. Run weekly retrospectives to identify patterns

The difference between a content assistant and an autonomous agent is the same as the difference between a calculator and a CFO.

One executes instructions. The other owns outcomes.

The shift from "AI helps me create" to "AI creates and learns" is happening faster than most people realize. Teams that figure out the architecture first will have compounding advantages.
